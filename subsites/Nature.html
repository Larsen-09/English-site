Nature

Scotland's flora and fauna

Thistle

The Thistle is a common name for a group of flowering plants typically covered in spikes, which it has to protect it from herbivores. With specifically the spear thistle being the national flower of Scotland.

Scottish crossbill

The Scottish Crossbill is a thick-set finch that has a large head and bill. Although its hard to distinguish from other finches, it serves as the United Kingdom's only endemic bird species.


Shetland mouse ear

The Shetland Mouse Ear is a flower endemic to Scotland that grows in tuffs. And the amounts on flower on it may vary wildly from year to year.

St kilda field mouse

St kilda field mouse The St Kilda Field Mouse is a kind of mouse that lives on the islands of St Kilda, which are now uninhabited by humans. Which leaves the St Kilda Field Mouse as the main inhabitant of the islands.


Scottish primrose

The Scottish Primrose is a type of flower endemic to the northern coast of Scotland. Only being a few centimeters in height even at full bloom, and the flower only being a handful of millimeters, the Scottish Primrose is a very small flower.

Orkney vole

The Orkney Vole is a kind of vole that’s endemic to the Orkney islands off the north coast of Scotland. And as these voles don't appear anywhere else in the British Isles, it is presumed that they appeared on these islands due to humans bringing them there.


St Kilda dandelion

The St Kilda Dandelion was discovered to be an unique species in 2012 when it was cultivated in the Royal Botanical Garden of Edinburgh, and was then found to have been growing on the islands of St Kilda for over 130 years.

Highland cattle

Highland Cattle is a form of cattle which originated in Scotland, with an especially long fur coat and long horns it is highly distinguishable from other forms of cattle. Having served as a core component of Scotland’s economy throughout history the Highland Cattle has now been exported to several parts of the world.
