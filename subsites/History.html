History

The history of Scotland

The Battle of Largs 1263.

The Battle of Largs was a major battle between the Norwegian and Scottish armies. But more importantly this battle and the legend that followed it is the reason why the thistle is the national flower of Scotland. The Battle of Largs took place in Largs, which is a small town on the west coast of Scotland. And this battle was fought between the Scots and the Norse Vikings.


The Battle of Bannockburn 1314.

Considered to be the most important battle of the first Scottish war for independence. The Battle of Bannockburn was a battle that took place in the summer of 1314 in the small village of Bannockburn, south of Stirling. This battle ended as a decisive victory for the Scottish and marked a major turning point of the war.


The Union of the Crowns 1603 / The Acts of Union 1707

The union of the crowns: This was the creation of the union between Scotland England and Ireland which unified them all under one crown.
The acts of union: This was the act that officially unified Scotland and England under one kingdom titled “Great Britain” which would later in 1800 become the “Kingdom of great Britain and Ireland” once Ireland joined, and then later on The United Kingdoms once most of Ireland declared independence.
